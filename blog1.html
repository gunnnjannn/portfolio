What Being a Class Representative has Taught Me About Leadership

Leadership isn’t about holding a title; it’s about taking responsibility. And for me, the journey started on the fifth day of orientation week. About 50 freshers from STME took an aptitude test. They came from BTech Computer Engineering, BTech AI & DS, and MBA Tech CE. Not for grades or competition. Instead, it's a role with equal challenge: Class Representative. Passing that test was the first step. The shortlisted candidates had interviews with the President, Vice Presidents, and a professor. This tested our communication skills and our ability to think under pressure

1. Leadership Requires Unwavering Resilience
One of the earliest lessons I learned was that leadership doesn’t always come with a smooth path. Sometimes, people misunderstand or twist the information. I had to step in to clarify and share the right message. To keep trust and credibility, actions matter more than words.

2. Teamwork is Crucial, But Not Always Guaranteed
In leadership, shared responsibility is ideal, but reality often differs. While some teammates step up, others may not meet expectations. In those moments, I took charge—managing my duties while ensuring the bigger goal stayed on track. Instead of worrying about setbacks, I looked for solutions. I stepped in when needed and kept things moving. This experience showed me that leadership goes beyond delegation. It's about taking ownership. When things don’t go as planned, true leaders adapt, make quick decisions, and guide the team forward. I learned to stay calm under pressure.

3. Communication Can Make or Break Leadership
Good communication builds strong leadership. It’s key for connecting students and faculty. Misunderstandings happen. To fix them, you need patience, clarity, and good listening skills. A leader should share messages. They must also encourage discussions that build collaboration, not division. Strong ties with faculty and open talks help everyone coordinate better.

>

4. Conflict Management is a Skill You Only Learn in Action
Every leadership role brings differing opinions and collaboration challenges. Navigating these without escalating tensions has been a crucial learning experience. Staying calm and making smart choices is important. This applies to aligning team expectations, addressing concerns, and resolving conflicts. Leadership means not shying away from disagreements. Instead, it’s about managing them well to ensure progress continues.

5. Leadership is About Finding Solutions, Not Dwelling on Problems
Frustration felt like the easy choice at times. But I learned that leadership isn't about seeing problems. It's about finding solutions. I didn't wait for perfect conditions. Instead, I took charge of what I could control. I adapted to challenges as they arose. True leadership comes from being resilient and solving problems. It means pushing ahead, even when things get tough.

Being a CR has been an eye-opening journey, despite the challenges. It has demonstrated strength and taught me to solve problems through careful planning. It also taught me the importance of focusing on the bigger picture. Each challenge has helped me grow. I’m excited to learn more on this journey.
